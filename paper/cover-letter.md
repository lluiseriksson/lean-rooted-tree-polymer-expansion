# Cover letter

Dear Editor,

Please consider the manuscript **“Marked Rooted-Tree Summation for Polymer Cluster Expansions with Holes: A Lean 4 Formalization”** by Lluis Eriksson.

The paper presents a machine-checked finite proof mechanism for target-sensitive bounds in a second Ursell expansion. Its main formalized ingredients are a normalized rooted child-factorial tree bound, a parent-normalized metric-moment recursion, a marked-root leaf summation, and a target-preserving geometric composition theorem. The complete artifact includes the paper, Lean 4 companion, exact dependency locks, CI, an axiom oracle, and an artifact-evaluation guide.

The manuscript makes a deliberately scoped claim. It formalizes finite combinatorics and geometry used in polymer cluster expansions with holes; it does not claim a construction of the raw four-dimensional Yang-Mills activity, a continuum limit, or a mass gap. We believe this clarity makes the work suitable for readers in formalized mathematics, constructive field theory, and rigorous statistical mechanics.

A clean artifact build compiles the publication-facing Lean endpoints and reconstructs the manuscript PDF. The exact source state, Lean toolchain, and Mathlib commit are frozen in the repository.

This manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere. The author has approved the submission and will provide any venue-specific declarations requested by the journal.

Sincerely,

Lluis Eriksson
