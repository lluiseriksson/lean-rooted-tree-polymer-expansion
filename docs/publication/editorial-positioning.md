# Editorial positioning

## Primary positioning

A formalization paper at the intersection of theorem proving, combinatorics,
and rigorous mathematical physics.

> **One-sentence pitch:** The artifact machine-checks the target-preserving proof
> order required to turn local factorial moment bounds into an explicit
> geometric second-Ursell estimate for polymer expansions with holes.

## Technically distinctive features

- The exact target union is retained until modified-metric decay is extracted.
- Parent--child incompatibility is retained until the local leaf sum is closed.
- A parent-metric normalization cancels exactly through rooted child counts.
- The `(n+1)` marking cost is canceled against the Ursell normalization and the
  rooted tree-profile estimate.
- Constants are explicit: root cost `M`, per-leaf ratio `4 M^2`.
- The paper, theorem map, provenance, and evaluator instructions are deployed as
  one versioned documentation site.

## Audience variants

### Formal methods and interactive theorem proving

Emphasize proof decomposition, immutable dependency pins, oracle output,
reusable finite combinatorics, and the prevention of invalid proof reorderings.

### Rigorous statistical mechanics and constructive QFT

Emphasize the target-sensitive second-Ursell mechanism, modified-metric holes,
rooted factorial moments, and the explicit geometric ratio.

### Artifact evaluation

Emphasize one-command builds, theorem-to-paper mapping, immutable source state,
and the short review path.

## Claims to avoid

- “Proof of the Yang--Mills mass gap.”
- “Closure of `hRpoly`.”
- “Continuum Yang--Mills construction.”
- “New infinity” or “new cardinality.”
- “Catalan theorem” unless a genuine Catalan refinement is later formalized.
