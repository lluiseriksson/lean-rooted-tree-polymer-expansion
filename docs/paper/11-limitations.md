# Limitations and open obligations

The theorem begins after a polymer weight has already been constructed.
A physical application to four-dimensional lattice Yang--Mills would
additionally require a model-specific chain such as $$\begin{gathered}
\text{gauge-fixed Hessian}\to\text{coercive precision}\to\text{localized covariance}\\
\to\text{fluctuation integral}\to\text{raw local activity}\to\text{source weight split}.
\end{gathered}$$ None of those identifications follows from the finite
theorem. In the present source-facing architecture, the remaining
analytic obligations include:

1.  an exact definition of the raw activity from the physical RG
    integral;

2.  a pointwise or normed activity estimate with modified-metric decay;

3.  the precise source-to-formal translation of constants and supports;

4.  integrability and measure-identification statements;

5.  the numerical smallness $4M^2\varepsilon<1$ in the physical
    parameter window.

Even discharging these would not by itself construct the continuum
theory. Thermodynamic and continuum limits, reflection positivity,
reconstruction, nontriviality, and a continuum spectral gap remain
separate problems.
