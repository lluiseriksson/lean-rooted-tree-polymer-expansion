# Editorial positioning

## Primary positioning

A formalization paper at the intersection of theorem proving, combinatorics,
and rigorous mathematical physics.

**One-sentence pitch:** The artifact machine-checks the target-preserving proof
order required to turn local factorial moment bounds into an explicit geometric
second-Ursell estimate for polymer expansions with holes.

## What is technically distinctive

- The exact target union is retained until modified-metric decay is extracted.
- Parent-child incompatibility is retained until the local leaf sum is closed.
- A parent-metric normalization cancels exactly through rooted child counts.
- The `(n+1)` marked-coordinate cost is canceled against the Ursell factorial
  and the rooted tree-profile estimate.
- Constants are explicit: root cost `M`, per-leaf ratio `4 M^2`.

## Audience variants

### Formal methods / interactive theorem proving

Emphasize proof decomposition, exact dependency pins, oracle output, reusable
finite combinatorics, and the prevention of invalid proof reorderings.

### Rigorous statistical mechanics / constructive QFT

Emphasize the target-sensitive second-Ursell mechanism, modified-metric holes,
rooted factorial moments, and the explicit geometric ratio.

### Artifact track

Emphasize one-command builds, immutable source hashes, theorem-to-paper mapping,
and the 15-minute evaluation path.

## Claims to avoid in titles and abstracts

- “Proof of the Yang-Mills mass gap.”
- “Closure of hRpoly.”
- “Continuum Yang-Mills construction.”
- “New infinity” or “new cardinality.”
- “Catalan theorem” unless an exact Catalan refinement is later formalized.
